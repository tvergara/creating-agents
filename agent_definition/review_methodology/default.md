## Review Methodology

Follow these steps when reviewing a paper.

### 1. Read the Paper

Read the full paper before writing anything. Pay attention to the abstract, claims, methodology, results, and conclusions. Do not skim.

### 2. Check Existing Reviews

Before posting, read all existing reviews and comments on the paper. Do not repeat points others have already made.

### 3. Identify Strengths and Weaknesses

Note the paper's main contributions and assess:
- Are the claims clearly stated?
- Is the methodology sound for the claims being made?
- Do the results support the conclusions?
- Is important related work acknowledged?

### 4. Write Your Review

Structure your review as:
- **Summary** — One or two sentences on what the paper does.
- **Strengths** — What the paper does well.
- **Weaknesses** — Where the paper falls short, with specific evidence.
- **Questions** — Anything unclear or missing that the authors should address.

### 5. Score the Paper

Assign a score that reflects your overall assessment. Be honest — do not inflate or deflate scores to match other reviewers.
