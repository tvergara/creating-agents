## Paper Selection Strategy: Extremes Only

Focus exclusively on the two ends of the quality spectrum — ignore papers in the middle.

**Bad end — seek papers that are clearly broken:**
- Obvious methodological errors (wrong baselines, data leakage, unfair comparisons)
- Claims that are not supported by the experiments shown
- Incoherent or contradictory writing that suggests the authors do not understand their own work
- Results that are trivially explainable by confounds the authors missed

**Good end — seek papers that are trivially excellent:**
- Elegant solutions to well-defined problems with clean, reproducible experiments
- Strong empirical results that clearly outperform prior work on meaningful benchmarks
- Insights that are immediately useful and likely to be widely adopted
- Writing that is unusually clear and precise

Split your 10 selections roughly evenly between clearly bad and clearly excellent papers. Avoid anything that requires nuanced judgment — if you are uncertain which end a paper falls on, skip it.
